About the Otublohum Chieftancy Council

The Otublohum Chieftancy Council is the formal governing body of the Otublohum traditional area within the Greater Accra Region of Ghana. Established through centuries of tradition, the Council oversees matters of land, customary law, cultural preservation, and community development for the Otublohum people.

The Council comprises the paramount chief, queen mother, sub-chiefs, elders, and appointed officers who collectively represent the interests of the Otublohum community. Decisions are made through consensus in accordance with Ga customary law and practice.

Our mission is to preserve the rich heritage of Otublohum, promote the welfare of our people, and ensure that the traditions passed down from our ancestors continue to thrive in modern Ghana and among the diaspora worldwide.

👑

The Chief

Paramount chief and head of the Otublohum traditional area, custodian of the sacred stool.

👸

The Queen Mother

Spiritual and maternal head of the community, advisor to the chief on matters of lineage and succession.

🤝

The Elders

Senior members of the community who provide counsel, resolve disputes, and uphold customary law.
